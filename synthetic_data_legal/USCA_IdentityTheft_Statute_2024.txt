Effective date: 2024-01-01
Section 1 — Scope
This statute revises California identity theft provisions to account for modern credential misuse and account takeover.
Section 2 — Definitions
•	Identifying information includes authentication credentials and device identifiers used to access accounts. 
•	Intent to benefit includes obtaining goods, services, or credit. 
Section 3 — Elements
Identity theft occurs when a person knowingly uses another’s identifying information without authorization to obtain benefit or facilitate fraud.
Section 4 — Penalty (Revised)
If convicted under this statute, the court may impose:
•	Imprisonment up to 5 years, and/or 
•	a fine up to $80,000, and/or 
•	restitution. 
Section 5 — Notes
This statute replaces prior maximum incarceration terms for California identity theft where inconsistent.
