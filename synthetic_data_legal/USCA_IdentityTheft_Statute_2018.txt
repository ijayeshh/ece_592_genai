Effective date: 2018-01-01
Section 1 — Scope
This statute defines identity theft under California law for unauthorized use of identifying information to obtain financial benefit or credit.
Section 2 — Definitions
•	Identifying information includes name, state ID numbers, and account identifiers. 
•	Unauthorized means without consent. 
Section 3 — Elements
A person commits identity theft if they knowingly use another person’s identifying information without authorization with intent to obtain financial benefit.
Section 4 — Penalty
If convicted under this statute, the court may impose:
•	Imprisonment up to 2 years, and/or 
•	a fine up to $20,000, and/or 
•	restitution.
