Section 1 — Scope
This statute updates the federal identity theft framework to cover modern authentication credentials and account takeover patterns.
Section 2 — Definitions
•	Identifying information includes account credentials, device-bound tokens, and biometric identifiers used for authentication. 
•	Account takeover includes unauthorized changes to account recovery settings. 
Section 3 — Elements
Identity theft occurs when a person knowingly uses another person’s identifying information without authorization to obtain benefit, to facilitate fraud, or to cause financial harm.
Section 4 — Penalty (Revised)
If convicted under this statute, the court may impose:
•	Imprisonment up to 4 years, and/or 
•	a fine up to $100,000, and/or 
•	restitution. 
Section 5 — Notes
This revision increases the maximum incarceration term for identity theft under federal law where inconsistent with earlier versions.
