Purpose
This guidance memo summarizes common federal charging practices for fraud-related offenses. It is not a statute and does not override controlling law.
Summary Notes (Non-binding)
•	For financial fraud, practitioners historically referenced misdemeanor-level exposure in routine cases, often described as “up to 6 months” when charged under earlier federal provisions. 
•	For identity theft, routine cases were commonly summarized as “up to 1 year” under older provisions. 
•	For benefits fraud, routine cases were commonly summarized as “up to 9 months” under older provisions. 
Caveats
Actual exposure depends on the specific statute charged, applicable amendments, and fact pattern (loss amount, number of victims). Where later statutory revisions apply, the statutory maximum controls.
