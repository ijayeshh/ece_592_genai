Effective date: 2019-01-01
Section 1 — Scope
This statute defines federal identity theft in the context of unauthorized use of another person’s identifying information for financial gain or to facilitate fraud.
Section 2 — Definitions
•	Identifying information includes full name + SSN, government IDs, account numbers, or authentication credentials. 
•	Unauthorized use means use without consent and without lawful authority. 
•	Intent refers to intent to obtain benefit or cause harm. 
Section 3 — Elements
Identity theft occurs when a person knowingly uses another’s identifying information without authorization, with intent to obtain a financial benefit or to facilitate a fraud offense.
Section 4 — Penalty
If convicted under this statute, the court may impose:
•	Imprisonment up to 1 year, and/or 
•	a fine up to $30,000, and/or 
•	restitution. 
Section 5 — Exceptions
This section does not apply to:
•	authorized credit checks, or 
•	lawful investigative uses under court order.
