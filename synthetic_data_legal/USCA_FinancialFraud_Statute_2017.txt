Effective date: 2017-01-01
Section 1 — Scope
This statute defines financial fraud under California law for state prosecution. It applies to intentional deception used to obtain money or credit in a financial transaction with a California nexus.
Section 2 — Definitions
•	Intent to defraud means intent to cause another to rely on a misrepresentation. 
•	Financial institution includes banks, credit unions, and state-regulated lenders. 
•	Transaction includes consumer credit applications and loan renewals. 
Section 3 — Elements
A person commits financial fraud if they knowingly misrepresent a material fact (or conceal a material fact when disclosure is required) with intent to obtain a financial benefit.
Section 4 — Penalty
If convicted under this statute, the court may impose:
•	Imprisonment up to 1 year, and/or 
•	a fine up to $10,000, and/or 
•	restitution. 
Section 5 — Conditions and Exceptions
This section does not apply when:
•	the misstatement is corrected before funds are disbursed; or 
•	the statement is immaterial and did not influence a decision. 
Section 6 — Notes
This statute is distinct from identity theft statutes and consumer protection civil remedies. A civil settlement does not automatically resolve criminal liability.
