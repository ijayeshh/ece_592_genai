Section 1 — Scope
This statute revises California’s financial fraud provisions to address evolving lending practices and digital documentation. It covers deception in financial transactions tied to California.
Section 2 — Definitions
•	Material means likely to influence a decision in a financial transaction. 
•	Financial benefit includes loan proceeds, credit line increases, and avoidance of repayment obligations. 
•	Digital submission includes electronic forms and scanned documents. 
Section 3 — Elements
Financial fraud occurs when a person knowingly engages in deception (false statement or material omission) with intent to obtain financial benefit and the deception is used in a covered transaction.
Section 4 — Penalty (Revised)
If convicted under this statute, the court may impose:
•	Imprisonment up to 3 years, and/or 
•	a fine up to $50,000, and/or 
•	restitution. 
Section 5 — Mitigation
Voluntary repayment prior to charge filing may be considered by the court but does not remove criminal liability.
Section 6 — Notes
This revision replaces the prior maximum incarceration term for financial fraud under California law where inconsistent.
